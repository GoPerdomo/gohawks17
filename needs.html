Team Needs

Offensive Line

The Offensive Line has changed completely from the one that has helped the Seahawks win the Super Bowl XLVIII. Once the NFL's most well-paid, it's now composed mostly by rookies, making it now the league's cheapest and most inexperienced OL. Even though Center Justin Britt begun showing signs of a bright future the rest of the group, which includes even a basketball-turned-footbal player, has still to prove its value.

Free Agency Update: The Seattle Seahawks signed Offensive Guards Luke Joeckel (former Jacksonville Jaguars) and Oday Aboushi (former Houston Texans).

Wide Receiver

Even with great receivers like the efficient Doug Baldwin and the speedster Tyler Lockett, the Seahawks still feels the need for adding a big, agressive target for Russell Wilson.

Running Back

Now that's an unfortunate thread. The Seattle Seahawks had until 2015 one of the best player on its backfield, Marshawn Lynch, aka Beast Mode. After his retirement the team has counted with Thomas Rawls and C.J. Prosise, that even though both had already great moments, they are still very young and inexperient.

Free Agency Update: The Seattle Seahawks signed Running Back Eddie Lacy (former Green Bay Packers). Lacy had a fantastic career with the Packers, but on the last couple years he gained a lot of weight and has shown difficulties to go back to his old form, harming his performance. The Seahawks signed him on a 1-year contract, including 7 different weight targets in which they will pay $55,000 bonus for each one that he reaches. In case he makes it and the team gets the Offensive Line straight, we can expect a great time in Seattle.
